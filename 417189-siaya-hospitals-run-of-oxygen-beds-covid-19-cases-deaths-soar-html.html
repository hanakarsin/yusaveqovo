Siaya Hospitals Run out Of Oxygen and Beds as COVID-19 Cases, Deaths Soar

Health facilities in Siaya county have run of oxygen even as COVID-19 cases and deaths continue to soar.

The fourth wave of the pandemic has taken a toll on residents in the region, with statistics from the county department of health showing at least one patient dies daily.

Siaya's 310 isolation ward is full to capacity and over 300 patients have been forced to remain under home-based care, The Standard reported.

Siaya county chief health officer Eunice Fwayo said community transmission was rampant over the six weeks.

“The death toll is devastating. Many of our patients do not have travel history and a number of sequencing has shown the dominance of the delta variant,” she said.

26 people buried on the same day

On Saturday, June 26, for instance, 26 people were buried on the same day within the same location.

These included nine members of St Anne’s Catholic Church, six at St Joseph Ugunja Catholic Church and 11 at St Pantaleon Siaya parish leaving priests worried.

Sega Parish priest Fred Ogambi expressed his concerns about the sudden high number of deaths.

"The deaths are sudden and the spike is worrying, with many succumbing to respiratory-related complications, as families have testified,” the cleric said.

Felix Okello, the coordinator of the COVID-19 response team at the parish described the deaths as 'abnormal'.

The official noted that previously they could bury two members on average per month but the situation had worsened.

Uhuru extends curfew in hotspot counties

On Tuesday, June 29, President Uhuru Kenyatta extended the nationwide dusk to dawn 7pm to 10pm curfew in the 13 counties, including Siaya declared COVID-19 hotspots.

At the same time, the head of state said curfew in the rest of the country would remain as usual, from 10pm to 4am for a further 60 days.

Speaking during a televised address on the COVID-19 situation in the country, Uhuru suspended political gatherings for another two months to curb the spread of COVID-19.

On matters of congregational worship, the president said it should be conducted with adherence to COVID-19 protocols and observance of the one-third capacity rule. However, this is in exception of the COVID-19 hotspot zone, where in-person worship was banned.
