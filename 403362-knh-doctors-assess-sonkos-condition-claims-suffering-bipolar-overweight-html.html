KNH doctors to assess Sonko's condition after claims he is suffering from bipolar, overweight

- Sonko was expected to appear in court for the hearing of KSh 10 million graft case against him

- He, however, failed to show up over what his lawyers said was an unstable medical condition

- Senior Counsel John Khaminwa said the former governor was suffering from bipolar, overweight and sleeping disorder

- The prosecution, however, dismissed the report and asked the court to issue a warrant of arrest against Sonko for missing proceedings

Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko on Wednesday, February 17, failed to appear in court again, due to what his lawyers described as deteriorating health condition.

Sonko was admitted to Nairobi Hospital about two weeks ago; at the time, numerous court cases were weighing heavily on his shoulders.

On Wednesday, the former governor was supposed to appear before anti-corruption court Chief Magistrate Douglas Ogoti for hearing of KSh 10 million graft case against him.

Unstable condition

However, he missed the session over what his lawyers stated was an unstable medical condition that could not allow him to appear in person.

Led by Senior Counsel John Khaminwa, the lawyers produced a medical report which indicated Sonko was suffering from bipolar, overweight and sleeping disorder.

However, Chief Prosecutor Taib Ali Taib dismissed the medical report arguing it did not indicate that the condition the accused was suffering from could not allow him to appear in court.

Derailing the case

Taib alleged the ex-governor was using sickness to derail proceedings of the case and asked the court to order his arrest.

“This report only says that Sonko is overweight, bipolar, can’t sleep, is allergic, and so no. It doesn't state that he cannot appear in court in person. These are nothing more than shenanigans by Sonko to seek to derail the hearing of this case," said the prosecutor.

Khaminwa protested the prosecutor's request to have their client arrested from the hospital, arguing Sonko had been arrested earlier for other charges and was currently in the state's hands.

"It is unheard of for a prosecutor to issue a warrant of arrest against someone who is already in custody. Sonko is sick and admitted to the hospital. He is in the hands of the Anti-Terrorism Police Unit Officers (ATPU). This is unheard of,“ the lawyer said.

KNH doctors

The court ordered a team of three doctors from Kenya National Hospital (KNH) to assess Sonko's medical condition and file a report in court before March 1.

The presiding magistrate also directed investigating officers in the KSh 10 million graft case to produce Sonko in court on March 4 for further directions.

The embattled politician is facing two other separate cases; one related to robbery with violence and assault charges, while in the other one, he is accused of working with terrorists.
