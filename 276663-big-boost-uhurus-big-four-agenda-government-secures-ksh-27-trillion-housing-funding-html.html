Big boost for Uhuru's Big Four agenda as government secures KSh 2.7 trillion housing funding

- The grant would be released in five years through Kenya Urban Support Programme

- At least 80,000 units would be constructed in 45 counties using the funds

- Kisumu county will be among first beneficiaries of the programme

- The county will receive KSh 780 million annually for the construction of low-cost houses.

- The government announced 30,000 houses would be constructed in Eastlands, Nairobi

President Uhuru Kenyatta's Big Four development agenda has received a major boost after the government secured a KSh 2.7 trillion World Bank grant to construct quality affordable houses across the country.

The grant, though conditional, would be released in a period of five years through the Kenya Urban Support Programme (KUSP) and will be used to construct 800,000 housing units in 45 counties.

TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported that Uhuru promised to prioritise construction of quality affordable homes, enhance food security, expand the manufacturing industry and provide universal health cover to all Kenyans in his last term of the presidency.

"Under this plan, the government intends to deliver one million homes in the next five years. Out of these, 800,000 will be one, two, and three-bedroom houses while the remaining 200,000 units will be social houses. Upgrading of slums will be covered under this programme," Housing and Urban Principal Secretary (PS) Charles Mwaura said.

In a press statement to TUKO.co.ke, Mwaura said the government had put in place stringent measures to ensure the funds would be specifically utilised on construction of quality affordable homes and urbanisation projects.

“One of the conditions is for the selected counties to have proper plans and functional town and city management boards,” he said.

According to the PS, all plans for the implementation of the project had been effectively concluded, with public participation expected to be conducted in the month of June 2018.

He said Kisumu County had already actualised plans for the project and would be among pacesetters in the implementation of the project highly valued by the president.

"Kisumu will receive KSh780 million annually for the construction of decent and low-cost houses," he said.

Speaking after a meeting with Kisumu Governor Anyang' Nyong’o on Monday, June 11, the PS said planning of the project would cover roads and other social amenities like schools, hospitals and market sheds among other public facilities.

Nyong'o said a city management board would oversee the implementation of the ambitious project.

"The project by the national government will complement another French government funded project with the county dubbed Kisumu Urban Project to change the face the lakeside city. Plans are underway to develop a Geo-plan for Kisumu to aid in the implementation of the two projects," the governor assured.

He said the county was completing the process of contracting a firm to do a complete geo-planning of Kisumu to guide it on where to put roads and how to zone habitation areas in a manner which would suit the houses construction project.

The national government has already announced plans to construct 30,000 houses in Nairobi's Eastlands area.

The plan will see 5,000 houses constructed in Shauri Moyo, 20,000 in Makongeni, 3,000 in Starehe and 2,000 in Park Road estate.
