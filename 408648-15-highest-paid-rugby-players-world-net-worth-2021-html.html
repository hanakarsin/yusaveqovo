15 highest paid rugby players in the world and their net worth 2021

Rugby is among the world's most played games, and the Rugby World Cup ranks third among the most-watched sporting events. However, it is not like other disciplines and sporting leagues like the Premier League, NBA, or NFL, where players' income information and net worth are readily available across media outlets. These massively talented professionals are popular globally, but it can be relatively hard to know who the highest paid rugby players in the world are.

Rugby is played globally, and teams compete at different levels, including club, national, and international levels. Every year teams work their way to the highest levels across different league systems, and the main event is Rugby World Cup. All these activities and immense game popularity attract TV deals and sponsorships that boost rugby players salary amounts.

The highest paid rugby players in the world

Rugby is one of the prestigious and high-paying disciplines, and most professional players are joining the list of the most paid sports personalities. Despite the disruption by the Coronavirus that halted most sporting events, these players continue extending their contracts, signing new ones, and making it big. Here are the highest paid rugby players in the world.

1. Dan Carter - £1 million

Dan Carter is believed to be the most successful fly-half to ever grace the rugby pitch, with most of his fans considering him to be the best rugby player in the world. He is a New Zealand national and plays for Racing 92. The star rakes in £1 million every year and is worth about £1.4million. On this list, he stands out as the highest paid rugby player.

2. Charles Puitau - £1 million

Charles is a former All Blacks' speedster who hit the headlines when he quit his international rugby ambitions prematurely while aged 23. But the move in 2015 was worth it as he entered a lucrative contract with PRO14's Ulster. Three years later, he joined Bristol Bears, a move that made him the first million-pound player in British rugby with an income of £1 million per season.

3. Stuart Hogg - £1 million

Stuart is a Scottish rugby player and captain of his national team. He hails from a family of talented rugby stars, and his excellence continues to earn him big money. While still playing for Glasgow Warriors, he was Scotland's best-paid sports personality. Stuart Hogg takes home more than £1 million every year.

4. Handre Pollard - €1.06 million (£920,000)

The Springboks playmaker and Rugby World Cup winner made headlines alongside Piutau as the richest rugby duo. This was after agreeing to join Top 14's Montpellier from Super Rugby's Bulls. The South African is currently worth over £1 million and earns €1.06 million per year.

5. Michael Hooper - $1.2 million (£870,000)

Michael Hooper is an Australian-born professional rugby player who currently serves as Super Rugby's Waratahs team captain. The talented player signed a lucrative five-year contract with Rugby Australia, making him among the players with the highest income globally. He earns about $1.2 million every year.

Steve Luatua also retired from international rugby ahead of an illustrious career to join RFU Championship's Bristol. He is currently the captain and a key player at the Bristol Bears. The former loose forward for the All Blacks signed a deal that makes him one of the highest-earning rugby professionals in the world. He takes home $1.33m every year.

7. Morgan Parra - $1.18m (£850,000)

Morgan quit playing for the French national team to focus on his half-back position at Massif Centrale club. The veteran France rugby union player can play outside-half and scrum-half and is also a reliable goal-kicker. Though his international career stalled, his recent contract extension with his current club makes him one of the top earners. He takes home $1.18m.

8. Johnny Sexton - $1.10m (£790,000)

Johnny hit the headlines in 2018 by grasping the "Rugby Player of the Year" title. After a brief season with Racing 92, the star went back to his native Leinster. He is the captain of the Ireland national team and Leinster and is currently the best-paid player in Ireland. He recently extended his contract and is worth $1.10m, making him one of the richest rugby players in his country.
