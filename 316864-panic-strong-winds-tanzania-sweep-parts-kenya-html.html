Panic as strong winds from Coastal region sweep across parts of Kenya

Strong winds accompanied by dust have swept several parts of the country leaving many in panic.

The dust storm was experienced in the coastal, central regions and Nairobi on Tuesday, October 1, leaving many shocked.

The Meteorological Department on its Twitter handle explained that what the country was experiencing was a "deep convectional system due to localised storm due to unstable atmosphere".

"What we are experiencing is a deep convective system in the southern section of Kenya. This system is moving from the Coastal Kenya as it propagates inland toward South East low lands, Nairobi and Central parts of Kenya," explained Met.

"The system is characterised by strong South easterly winds of about 15-20 Knots, updraft and downdraft winds with heavy convective rains," it added.

Motorist were cautioned against driving into or through the dust storm wile those in the dust already were adviced to turn their headlights, slow down and safely pull over.

People with respiratory and cardiovascular conditions such as asthma, bronchitis and emphysema were asked to stay indoors to avoid dust particles getting deep into their lungs.

Besides causing panic among Kenyans, the winds also left behind a trail of damage after a number of trees and billboards were reported to have been blown away by the winds.

A dust storm, also called sandstorm, is a meteorological phenomenon common in arid and semi-arid regions that arise when a gust front or other strong wind blows loose sand from the surface.

Dry lands around North Africa and the Arabian peninsula are the main terrestrial sources of airborne dust.
