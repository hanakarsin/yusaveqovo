Top 4 Race: Ruthless Leicester Thrash Baggies to Tighten Grip on 3rd Place

- Leicester City proved too strong for West Brom on Thursday, April 22

- The Foxes are now four points clear off Chelsea in fourth place

- Goals from Jamie Vardy, Ihenacho and Johny Evans were enough to see off the Baggies, who are in danger of being relegated

Leicester City were in ruthless form on Thursday, April 22, as they thrashed a relegation-threatened West Brom side to move four points clear in third place.

Three first-half goals were enough to see Brendan Rodgers’s side claim all three points, leaving West Brom with a real possibility of relegation.

Jamie Vardy scored the opener after being teed up by Timothy Castagne before defender Johnny Evans nodded in a deflected corner to make it 2-0.

In-form Nigerian striker Kelechi Ihenacho then made it 3-0 with Vardy turning provider after a darting run down the left.

The game had several chances and West Brom came closest when Matheus Pereira hit the woodwork in the first half.

In the last few weeks, there were concerns that Leicester would start to slip up in the top four race, especially after defeats to West Ham and Manchester City.

However, as has been the case for a large chunk of the season, the Foxes showed immense character against underdogs West Brom who are themselves fighting for Premier League survival.

The win takes the Foxes four points clear of Chelsea, who occupy the final Champions League spot with six games remaining.

For West Brom, it seems like Sam Allardyce’s record of never being relegated from the Premier League is coming to an end as they stay nine points adrift of safety with games running out.

The Baggies also have a far inferior goal difference, even if they were to catch up to Burnley in 17th place.

Speaking after the game, Rodgers played down his top four opponents, saying he was fully focused on his own team.

"I am not thinking about the other teams. We have to think about ourselves. We can only control ourselves," he said."This group will sometimes lose games but they are so honest and they want to be better. When you have that it is the dream ticket. It is all you can ask for as a coach."
