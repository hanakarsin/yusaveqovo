Laikipia Woman rep chastises Ann Waiguru for not getting pregnant after wedding

- Waruguru said the recent Kibra mini-poll does not mean the Building Bridges Initiative will succeed

- The woman rep ridiculed Kirinyaga governor Ann Waiguru for not bearing children months after marriage

- The lawmaker said the governor was still an amateur in politics

Laikipia Women Representative Cate Waruguru has hurled unsavoury sentiments to Kirinyaga Governor Ann Waiguru saying she is yet to give birth even after her wedding to lawyer Kamotho Waiganjo.

The visibly angry legislator was disgusted by the governor's support for the Building Bridges Initiatives (BBI) and told her to first give birth before thinking of the initiative.

Speaking during the Inua Mama rally in Uasin Gishu county on Sunday, November 10, the Laikipia lawmaker said the governor was still green in politics as she was a first-time governor.

"Let me remind Waiguru that just because she was fronted by the Jubilee party, it doesn't mean she is the Wangu Makeri of the Mt.Kenya region," an angry Cate said.

The women representative also said the recent Orange Democratic Party's victory in the Kibra mini-poll does not mean the BBI will also succeed.

She further dared the governor to resign from her gubernatorial seat and bring an ODM candidate to vie for the seat and test whether the party will win.

She spoke barely three days after the Raila led party scooped the Kibra parliamentarian seat after Imran Okoth emerged the winner.

Waiguru, On Sunday, November 3, had gone to Kibra to drum up campaign for Imran despite the Jubilee party having its own candidate.
