Joseph Chebii: Baringo Chief Who Opted to Walk to Nairobi to Meet Uhuru over Stadium Arrives

  • Joseph Chebii arrived in Nairobi on Monday, September 6, after the historical walk which he commenced on Tuesday, August 24
  • Chebii is set to meet top-level government officials over his stadium proposal
  • The former chief said the majority of the youths in the region had resorted to substance abuse and related crimes due to idleness

A former chief in Baringo county who resolved to trek to Nairobi to meet President Uhuru Kenyatta has finally arrived in Kenya's capital.

Joseph Chebii, a retired Kimoso location chief, said he embarked on a 354 km journey to the city to seek Uhuru's help in building a modern sports ground in his Kabarnet area.

Chebii arrived in Nairobi on the evening of Monday, September 6, after the historical walk which he commenced on Tuesday, August 24.

Sources privy to the former administrator's long trek disclosed that he is set to meet top-level government officials over his stadium proposal.

For instance, Chebii is scheduled to meet Sports Cabinet Secretary Amina Mohamed on Wednesday, September 8.

Idleness

In a past interview, the former chief said the majority of the youths in the region had resorted to substance abuse and related crimes due to idleness.

"The only challenge on the road is food, but I can't go look for food. I rely on honey and water to keep me moving. That is what is important now," he stated.

The 61-year-old said he hopes to receive a warm reception at the State House Nairobi and return home with good news.

"On Tuesday, I did a very long kilometre, about 40 km. Yesterday was 20km. I'm hopeful the stadium will be built. I do not have any other agenda to discuss with him. We need a stadium for the future generation," he added.
