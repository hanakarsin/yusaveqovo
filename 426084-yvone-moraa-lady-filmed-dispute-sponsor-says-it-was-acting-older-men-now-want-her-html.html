Yvonne Moraa: Lady Filmed in Dispute with Presumed Sponsor Says It Was Acting, Older Men Now Want He

  • Since then, Yvonne Moraa, the lady who was being 'manhandled' by the man, said that she had received all manner of insults

A video showing a young lady and an older man having a dispute during their perceived romantic date went viral on social media about a month ago.

After generating mixed reactions, it later emerged that the clip was extracted from an Ekegusii drama series dubbed Bosongo Chituka, which aires on Ndizi TV.

Since then, Yvonne Moraa, the lady who was being 'manhandled' by the man, told Nation that she had received all manner of insults, with some of her friends abandoning her despite being a stage-managed act.

Men want me

On the flip side, Moraa said other men got the wrong impression of her and tried to lure her into a romantic relationship thinking she entertained sponsors.

The upcoming actress said her friends leaked her phone number and since then, she has been receiving numerous calls from men inviting her for dates.

She disclosed that some men were willing to send her fare to visit them, something she finds so disgusting.

"It is just disgusting to see how some men treat young innocent girls. I just acted in the series, now people are treating me like the character I played. I am just trying to make a living in a legitimate manner through art,” said Moraa.

Moraa lamented that she had had to pay the price for her role in the drama as society judges a woman harshly.

The actress disclosed that she was a nursing student at Kenya Medical Training College (KMTC) in Migori and was trying to raise her fee through acting.

Sponsors vs young men

In other news, comedian turned radio presenter Jalang’o also known as Jalas begged sponsors not to splash money on young men’s girlfriends.

According to the radio host, sponsors should spoil women with moderation to avoid embarrassing the unsuspecting boy child.

During his morning show, the hilarious chap claimed he is well aware there is a war brewing between struggling youths and elderly sponsors.

“We know there is a war. We know that the fourth world war is on. Sponsor is someone who takes your woman somewhere you have never been, cannot be,” Jalang’o joked.
