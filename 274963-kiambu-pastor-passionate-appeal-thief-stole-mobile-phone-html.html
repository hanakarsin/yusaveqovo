Kiambu pastor makes passionate appeal to thief who stole his mobile phone

- Pastor Macharia resorted to publishing posters in a bid to ask a thief to return his stolen phone

- The man of God lost his phone, an Itel A31, while in a matatu plying the Wangige-Kikuyu route

- Macharia offered to buy the thief a new phone should he return his

- The pastor claimed he suspects a man who was seated next to him in the matatu took his phone

- He said his phone had several sermons and crucial contacts of people he was counselling

A Kiambu based man of the clothe has become an instant online sensation after pleading with whoever stole his phone to return it in one of the most hilarious ways.

Pastor Geoffrey Macharia was forced to publish several posters urging the thief to return his phone and distributed the fliers to residents of Muthure, Rukubi and nearby areas in Kiambu county.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt that Pastor Macharia lost his phone on Monday, May 21, while in a matatu plying the Wangige-Kikuyu route.

According to Macharia, his phone may have been stolen by a man who was seated besides him in the matatu.

"I lost my phone on Monday, May 21, at around 2.00pm while in a matatu plying the Wangige-Kikuyu route. I think whoever stole my phone is a man who was seated next to me. I realised I had lost my Itel A31 after alighting,'' the poster read.

Macharia, in one of the posters written in Kiswahili, went ahead to reveal the phone contained very important church information that he couldn't afford to lose.

The pastor held that a lot of things could go wrong on his side should he not get the phone back.

''I am a pastor at Worldwide Mission Church in Gachie and the phone had a lot of important church information, sermons and I also use it to help people struggling with alcoholism. It also contains a lot of messages from countless people I have been helping,'' Macharia added.

The man of God wrapped up by asking the thief and whoever might know who has the phone to contact him and return the phone, revealing that he was ready to award them.

He added that he was ready to buy the thief a new phone should he return the one he stole from him.

Macharia held that he wished he could let the phone go because it was not that expensive but he cannot because of the important messages and data it carries.

''I am asking whoever took my phone to return it and God will bless him. I'm ready to reward him and even buy a new phone for him as appreciation. Please help me pass the message to family and friends," the pastor further said.

The posters cracked quite a multitude of people on social media, considering the fact that no one has ever published such asking thieves to return their belongings, especially phones.
