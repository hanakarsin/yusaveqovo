Radio host Joyce Gituro discloses ex-hubby left her, took everything from their house

- According to the Radio Jambo presenter, her hubby started changing after they joined a new church which was very weird to her

- The mother of three said at one time, he took a lot of money to buy a TV for the pastor yet they had school fees arrears for their children

- After things got heated between them, her husband one day came home and took all their household items and left

Journalist Joyce Gituro has opened up on how she lost her husband of many years after he decided to join a new church.

Speaking during a one on one with Jeridah Andayi, the mother of three said things changed when she and her religious lover changed the church they used to attend.

Gituro said her husband had gotten into a cult and she was not ready to be part of his beliefs and this affected their union the more.

"The church was the main reason me and my husband parted ways. I lost my husband in church. My husband told me we had to leave the church where we used to worship and he took to a church that I did not believe in. When I went there I told my husband I am not comfortable with the doctrines...The church was sounding funny," she narrated.

The mother of three recounted a day they were asked to close the door and the windows of the new church to cast out the devil.

"I remember one day the pastor said we close the windows and doors so that we chase out the devil. I told my husband I am not very comfortable and went out with my kids. When I walked out the pastor did not like it and that is where the problems with my hubby began... When I talked to my hubby, he reprimanded me that walking out was wrong," she added.

The Radio Jambo presenter said her hubby left them and carried everything from their house.

"My hubby left us. He carried everything in the house. I have never seen a man who when leaving packs the cooker, fridge, seats, curtains and other things in the house. I was shocked. We had our own issues but I did not expect it to get to that," said the religious woman.

Gituro said she started seeing some weird behaviors in her hubby who would always receive scary prophecies from the pastor.

She recalled one day he took a lot of money to buy a TV for the pastor at a time they had not paid their children's school fees.

"I called my parents-in-law. I told them that their son had joined a cult. He would disappear saying he has gone for prayers. The kids would see him and the cycle continues," added Gituro.

The radio presenter recalled one time his now ex-hubby came from his usual prayers and told her that the kids were not his.

"I remember one time he came back from his usual prayers and said the kids did not belong to him. My hubby got into debts. I suspect he was taking the money to this church. He got into debts to an extent that even my car was taken away," she said.

The mother of three said that one day she was followed by the police with the claims that her car had been stolen.

"I was followed by police all the way to Royal Media. The police asked me if the car was mine and I told them yes. They told me that the car had been stolen. I was confused because I had given my hubby money to buy me the car. I later realised that he did not buy me a car but hired one and brought it to me. It was a stolen car," she added

In a related story, media personality Margaret Wariko revealed what led to her divorce at such a young age.

In an interview with Monica Kagoni, Wariko disclosed she said 'I Do' to her college sweetheart at the age of 23.

According to the beautiful mom of one, she was a reserved person and on completion of her diploma, she felt that she was ready to be a wife.

Wariko disclosed that at the time of marriage, she and her lover had already secured themselves jobs and were ready to soldier on.

"I got married at 23. At the time I was working. I was in the same college as my ex-hubby. At the time he had no money but really looked serious. I was focussed on how he was committed and I decided to give it a try. We dated for close to three years and adjusted my life to fit in his character because I saw he was a good man," said the Mediamax employee.

According to the 31-year-old lady, her marriage was okay at the start but things changed when they welcomed their child.

Wariko disclosed her then hubby never wanted to go out with her but he would hang out with his friends.
