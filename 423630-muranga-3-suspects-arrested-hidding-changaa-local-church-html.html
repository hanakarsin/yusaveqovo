Murang'a: 3 Suspects Arrested while Hidding Chang'aa in Local Church

  • Cleophas Juma said officers found the five suspects at Gikindu village while busy distilling the illicit liquor
  • The police officers were acting on a tip-off from the residents who had complained that the illegal trade was happening within the church's precincts
  • Suspects Anthony Ngugi Mwangi, Peterson Macharia Kihara, and Kelvin Mwangi Ng'ang'a appeared before the Kigumo Law Courts and pleased guilty

Police officers have arrested three male suspects who were found distilling chang’aa and had hidden 75 litres of it in a church compound in Murang'a county to avoid detection.

Liquor in Gitindu village

The officers, who were acting on a tip-off, also seized 101 stones of bhang from two female suspects and KSh 2,340 they had earned as proceeds of the trade.

According to area police boss Cleophas Juma, officers found the suspects, Anthony Ngugi Mwangi, 32, Peterson Macharia Kihara, 30, and Kelvin Mwangi Ng'ang'a, 28, at Gikindu village while busy at work distilling the illicit liquor.

Cleophas said several faithful residents had complained that the illegal trade was happening within the church's precincts since police officers rarely search such compounds.

The suspects appeared before Senior Principal Magistrate Shivai Agade at the Kigumo Law Courts.

They pleaded guilty to the manufacturing, storing and marketing of the illicit brew.

Mwangi was found to be the mastermind of the crime and was fined KSh 100,000 or serve 12 months in prison in default.

Kihara was fined KSh 20,000 or serve six months in prison.

Ng'ang'a was charged as an accomplice in the crimes and was fined KSh 20,000 or six months in prison in default.

Following the arrest, Juma urged local youths to reach out to the government for help with starting small enterprises instead of wasting their lives and those of their fellow age mates.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that a Trans Nzoia police corporal was apprehended after being caught red-handed ferrying charcoal and aloe vera using a police lorry.

Gilbert Mbilivili was caught transporting seven bags of charcoal and 65 bags of aloe vera on a police lorry along the Kapenguria-Kitale Road without proper documents.
