Atwoli Road: COTU Boss Condemns Vandalism on Road Signpost Days After Its Launch

- Atwoli likened the vandalism to an act of anarchy and impunity

- He said police would be engaged to established the individuals behind the act

- The road was renamed after Atwoli to honour his dedication to serving workers

The secretary-general to Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) Francis Atwoli has detested the vandalisation done on the signpost to his road less than a week into its launch.

Amateur images made rounds on Sunday, May 30, showing the signpost having been brought down by unknown individuals.

A section of Kenyans on Twitter celebrated the act, with others joining Atwoli in condemning those behind it.

"Kenyans are RISING UP! We shall protect our roads and streets from vile road signs!" said Boniface Mwangi, an activist.

The COTU boss called out the vandals, adding police were on course to identify them.

"Vandalization is impunity and primitiveness. In a country that believes in the Rule of Law, there are procedures and processes to be followed when unsatisfied. I am against anarchy. However, the police will look into that," Atwoli said.

The renaming of the Kileleshwa's Dig Dig Road to Francis Atwoli Road on Thursday, May 27, was authorised by the Nairobi county government, which said Atwoli's dedication in fighting for workers' rights motivated the move.

Speaking at the function and in the presence of the capital's acting governor Anne Kananu, Atwoli thanked the county's leadership for recognising his role in the trade movement.

Appreciating the gesture by the county, Atwoli reaffirmed his commitment to addressing the plight of Kenyan workers.

"I want to thank the leadership of Nairobi county, under Anne Kananu, for this great honour and recognition of services to the government of the day, the community and workers at large. We will continue offering our unwavering support to the leadership of the county," he stated.

The road became the latest to be named after a public figure.

The move to rename the rad after the COTU boss rubbed netizens the wrong way, taking to Twitter to unleash their discomfort.

"Why now? My beloved Kenya, why? For how long will we continue having roads and buildings being named after individuals? This is backwardness. Why not name it Tharaka road, Baragoi road, Lunabo road, Nyando road.... Are we short of names in Kenya," Munna Eric said.
