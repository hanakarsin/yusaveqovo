Details of 3 women who attacked Mombasa police station

– Three suspected female terrorists were shot dead by police after they attempted to set Mombasa police station on fire using a petrol bomb on Sunday, September 11

- The women had apparently gone to rescue a terror suspect, a former Recce squad officer, who had been held at the station

Details about the three suspected female terrorists who attacked Mombasa police station have emerged.

The three were shot dead, on Sunday, September 11, after they attempted to rescue a terror suspect who had been held at the Mombasa police station.

According to police sources, they stormed the station not knowing that the said suspect had been moved to Nairobi a day earlier.

Below are the details about the three young women believed to be members of the Jaysh Al Ayman terror cell based in Boni Forest, Lamu. The group is associated with the Somalia-based Al-Shabaab.

1. Tasnim Yakub Abdullahi Farah

She is believed to have been the one who led the attack.

Tasnim sat for her Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams in 2015 at Qubaa Muslim School in Mombasa and scored a B- (Minus). Police say she was in her early 20s (below 25 years of age).

2. Fatma Omar Yusuf

The second woman, Fatma Omar, is said to have been 23-years-old. She did her KCSE exams at Coast Girls High School in 2013.

3. Mariam
