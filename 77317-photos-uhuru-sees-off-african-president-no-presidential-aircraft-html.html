PHOTOS: Liberia's Ellen Johnson Sirleaf Has No Presidential Aircraft

Many countries in the world are known to have special aircraft for their leaders, some so posh that they are given special names and are always escorted by military jets wherever they go to protect the leaders from any possible danger.

READ ALSO: 5 Mind Blowing Facts About Air Force One, The Jet Obama Uses

One such country is the United States of America whose presidential jet is known as Air Force One. It is one of the most unique and state-of-the-art aircraft in the world today and is specially designed to fly the US president whenever he leaves the country.

The craft made headlines when it touched down at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in July, 2015, when US President Barack Obama visited the country.

The craft is always escorted by military jets that can evacuate the most powerful man in the world in case of any danger.

It is therefore surprising to learn that Liberia's President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf has no presidential jet.

READ ALSO: 8 Things About Kenya’s Presidential Jet – Harambee One

This was revealed when the first woman to be president in Africa was escorted to the JKIA by President Uhuru Kenyatta upon completion of her one-week visit to Kenya.

The Liberian president was seen boarding a Kenya Airways plane, effectively revealing that she probably has no presidential jet unlike most African and world leaders.

Kenya is one of the many countries that have presidential jets.

Sirleaf has been in the country for a week where she was attending the ongoing World Trade Organisation Summit at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC).

She managed to lobby for the inclusion of Liberia in the World Trade Organisation (WTO).
