Idd-Ul-Adha: List of Other Public Holidays Kenyans are Expecting in 2021

  • Idd-Ul-Adha, loosely translated as a festival of sacrifice, is an Islamic festival
  • On October 10, Kenyans will mark Huduma Day which was renamed from Moi Day
  • On December 12, Kenyans will celebrate Jamuhuri day

About a week ago, Interior and Coordination of National Government Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i declared Tuesday, July 20, a public holiday to mark Idd-Ul-Adha.

Idd-Ul-Adha, loosely translated as a festival of sacrifice, is an Islamic festival to remind Muslims of the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim to offer his son as a sacrifice as ordered by Allah (God).

Being a national holiday, Kenyan workers especially those in the public service are exempted from their official duties.

With five months and some days remaining until the end of 2021, Kenyans still have a number of public holidays to mark.

They include:

1. Huduma Day

Huduma Day is a public holiday observed on October 10 each year to honour the second president of Kenya Daniel Arap Moi.

The holiday was renamed from Moi day. With the anniversary falling on a Sunday this year, it will be observed the following Monday, October 11.

2. Mashujaa Day

About 10 days after celebrating Huduma Day, Kenyans will celebrate Mashujaa Day.

The Wednesday, October 20 holiday will honour all Kenyans who contributed to the struggle for Kenya's independence.

3. Diwali

On Thursday, November 4, Kenyans will join other people in the world in celebrating Diwali.

Diwali is a festival of lights and is majorly observed by Hindus.

4. Jamhuri Day

Jamhuri Day is a national holiday celebrated on December 12 each year.

The holiday is meant to officially mark the date when Kenya became a republic on December 12 1964, just one year and six months after gaining independence.

With the celebration falling on a Sunday this year, the day will be honoured on Monday, December 13.

5. Christmas Day

On this day, over two billion people in the world commemorate the birth of Jesus Christ.

The day marks the nativity of Jesus which, according to tradition took place on December 25 1 BC.

6. Utamaduni Day

Utamaduni Day was renamed from Boxing Day and is celebrated on December 26.

The day was set aside to mark the country’s rich cultural diversity and heritage.

This year, the public holiday will be observed on Monday, December 27.
