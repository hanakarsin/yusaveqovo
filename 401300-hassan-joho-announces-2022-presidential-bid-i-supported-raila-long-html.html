Hassan Joho announces 2022 presidential bid: &quot; I have supported Raila for long&quot;

Mombasa governor Ali Hassan Joho has declared interest in the 2022 presidency seat.

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) deputy party leader said he is ripe for the country's top job and has enough experience to steer the nation forward.

Speaking on Thursday, January 28, at Kisumu Ndogo in Nyali sub-county while officially handing over title deeds to residents, the politician nicknamed the Sultan argued he had supported former prime minister Raila Odinga for a long time and as such it was time for him to reciprocate.

"I have stood with ODM for a long period of time. I have supported Raila Odinga for long and I want to send a message out for them to understand that I am now in the race to State House using the ODM ticket," Joho declared.

Joho backs rotational presidency concept

Joho said the presidency was not meant for the selected few and insisted it was time for small communities to take over the leadership of the country.

"It is not possible for someone to think the presidency is meant for just a few selected people. Even some of us who come from small tribes have a right to contest because we got the experience and the zeal to lead too. With the help of God, we will be in the race until the last minute," Joho insisted.

His announcement came after his counterpart in the ODM party, Wycliffe Oparanya, who is also the Kakamega governor asked Raila to back him in 2022 presidential election.

Oparanya seeks Raila's support

The Council of Governors boss said he has supported the former premier all through and it was time for him to return the favor by endorsing his bid.

Oparanya said he will beat Deputy President William Ruto hands down if he gets the outfit's presidential ticket.

Suba South Member of Parliament John Mbadi had revealed the Opposition supremo would be in the State House race to succeed his handshake partner President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Raila, however, is yet to publicly declare whether he would run for presidency but his elder brother Oburu Odinga previously hinted he would be in 2022 succession matrix after getting the support of the so called system and the deep state.
